Southern Fried Catfish

Description

Southern Fried Catfish is easy to make and this restaurant-quality recipe won’t disappoint! You’ll love this 3-step, 7-ingredient recipe and it’s ready in less than 20-minutes! The crispy fish coating contains no egg or flour so if you’re on a gluten-free diet, this fish is for you!

Ingredients

  • 4 fillets catfish
  • 1½ cups oil vegetable, canola or peanut oil
  • 2 cups cornmeal yellow
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Pat fillets with paper towel
  2. Place fish in a large baking dish or plastic storage bag, pour in buttermilk; coat well; allow to sit for 10-minutes in refrigerator
  3. In a large bowl, add cornmeal, salt, pepper and cayenne; stir with fork till blended
  4. Using a cast-iron, or heavy bottom skillet, pour oil into skillet and heat to medium
  5. While oil is heating, take a fillet directly from the buttermilk and place it in the cornmeal; pat the cornmeal on the fillet; coat it all over, twice; repeat with remaining fillets
  6. Cooking in batches of two fillets, place them in the hot oil; DO NOT MOVE THE FILLETS until they've cooked for 2 1/2 - 3 minutes on one side
  7. After 2 1/2 -3 minutes, use tongs and flip the fish over and cook for an additional 3 minutes
  8. Remove fillets from oil and place on a paper towel
  9. Repeat until all fillets are cooked and drained
  10. Repeat until all fillets are cooked and drained
